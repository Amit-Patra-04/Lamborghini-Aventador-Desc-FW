The Aventador Legacy

Born in 2011, the Lamborghini Aventador marked a new era in the world of super sports cars. With its revolutionary design and groundbreaking technology, it redefined what was possible in automotive engineering.

The Aventador represents the pinnacle of Lamborghini's engineering prowess, combining raw power with unmatched precision and control. Its iconic silhouette and scissor doors have become synonymous with automotive excellence.

Performance Features

Engineering excellence that defines the Aventador's capabilities

Lamborghini Dynamic Steering

The LDS system adapts the steering ratio based on speed and selected driving mode, providing precise control at high speeds and easy maneuverability at low speeds.

Magnetorheological Suspension

The adaptive suspension system uses magnetically-controlled dampers that can adjust their stiffness in milliseconds, balancing comfort and performance.

Drive Mode Selector

The ANIMA system offers multiple driving modes – Strada, Sport, and Corsa – each calibrating the engine, transmission, steering, suspension, and stability control for different driving experiences.

Power Distribution

The Aventador's intelligent all-wheel-drive system dynamically distributes power between the front and rear axles, optimizing traction in all driving conditions while maintaining the car's dynamic handling characteristics.

Engineering Excellence

The technical innovations that define the Aventador

Carbon Fiber Monocoque

The Aventador's carbon fiber monocoque represents a revolutionary approach to supercar construction, providing exceptional rigidity while minimizing weight.

Single-Piece Construction

Unlike traditional chassis designs, the Aventador’s monocoque is manufactured as a single piece, eliminating joints and connection points that can compromise structural integrity.

Weight Optimization

Weighing just 147.5 kg (325 lbs), the carbon fiber monocoque provides exceptional strength-to-weight ratio, contributing to the Aventador’s impressive performance metrics.

Crash Protection

The monocoque’s design incorporates advanced crash structures that absorb and distribute impact forces, providing exceptional occupant protection without compromising performance.

Aerodinamica Lamborghini Attiva (ALA)

Lamborghini's patented active aerodynamics system represents a breakthrough in supercar design, providing adaptive downforce and drag reduction based on driving conditions.

Active Air Intake System

Electronically controlled flaps in the front splitter and rear wing can open or close in less than 500 milliseconds, optimizing airflow for either maximum downforce or reduced drag.

Aero Vectoring

The ALA system can independently adjust downforce on the left and right sides of the rear wing, improving cornering stability and reducing understeer at high speeds.

Performance Impact

The ALA system contributed significantly to the Aventador SVJ's record-setting Nürburgring lap time of 6:44.97, demonstrating its effectiveness in real-world performance scenarios.
